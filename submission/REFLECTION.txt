REFLECTION — Air-Raid Alert Risk Classification

What went wrong?
My first design forecast exact alert times with a random train/test split. Both
were wrong: exact timing is false precision, and random splits leak the future
into the past in a time series. A second, subtler leak appeared mid-build — the
decision threshold was being tuned on the test set.

How did I adjust?
I reframed it as 3-hour-ahead classification with a strict chronological
train/validation/test split, moved threshold tuning to validation, and added
automated leakage checks. When the model came out near-random, I hypothesised the
missing signal was geographic propagation and tested neighbour features across ten
regions.

Why is the final version better?
It is leakage-free, honestly validated, and produces a real finding — western
regions are predictable, direct targets are not — reproducing published work
instead of an inflated score.
