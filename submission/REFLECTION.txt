REFLECTION — Air-Raid Alert Risk Classification

What went wrong?
My first design forecast exact alert times and used a random train/test split.
Both were wrong: exact-minute timing is false precision and near-impossible to
validate, and a random split leaks the future into the past in a time series.
A second, subtler leak appeared mid-build — the decision threshold was being
tuned on the test set.

How did I adjust?
I reframed the task as 3-hour-ahead binary classification with a strictly
chronological train/validation/test split, moved threshold tuning to the
validation block, and added automated leakage checks. When the model came out
near-random, I hypothesised the missing signal was geographic propagation,
added neighbouring-region features, and tested across ten regions.

Why is the final version better?
It is leakage-free (proven by automated audit), honestly validated on recent
held-out data, and it produces a real finding: western/peripheral regions are
predictable and benefit from spatial features, while direct targets like Kyiv
are not — a result that reproduces published work rather than an inflated score.
